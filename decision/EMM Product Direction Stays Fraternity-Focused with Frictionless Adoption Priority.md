# EMM Product Direction Stays Fraternity-Focused with Frictionless Adoption Priority

## Context

During the Portfolio Narrative and Resume System Planning session (April 2026), the direction for Executive Mind Matrix (EMM) was explicitly clarified after 15 clarifying questions were answered — including questions about competitive differentiation against established project management tools like Jira.

## Options Considered

1. **Broaden scope** — position EMM against general PM tools (Jira, etc.) and expand target market beyond fraternities
2. **Stay fraternity-focused** — maintain narrow scope for fraternal organization use case, prioritize frictionless adoption over feature breadth

## Decision

Stay fraternity-focused. Prioritize frictionless adoption over feature parity with broader PM tools.

## Rationale

The competitive differentiation question against Jira was flagged as an open issue but not resolved in this session — it was deferred for future analysis. The decision to stay narrow avoids premature scope expansion and optimizes for adoption friction in the specific context where EMM has a natural foothold.

## Consequences

EMM product design and positioning work should be evaluated against fraternity adoption patterns, not enterprise PM tool benchmarks. The Jira comparison question remains open (a task was created for it) and should be revisited before any scope expansion decision. Product bets that increase complexity at the expense of frictionless onboarding are out of scope under this direction.
