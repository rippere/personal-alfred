# Fasciculus Gracilis Is Absent Above T1 in the Posterior Funiculus

## Constraint
The fasciculus gracilis (FG), the medial division of the dorsal column carrying lower body (leg and lower trunk) fine touch and proprioception, is only present in the posterior funiculus at spinal levels T1 and below. Above T1, the posterior funiculus contains only the fasciculus cuneatus (upper limb and upper trunk inputs). Lesion analysis and clinical imaging must account for this somatotopic boundary.

## Source
Spinal cord cross-sectional anatomy, NEURO 302 and NEURO 404 lectures. Explicitly noted in class: "FG is in the posterior funiculus — only present at levels below T1."

## Implications
A dorsal column lesion above T1 affects only upper limb fine touch and proprioception via the fasciculus cuneatus; the fasciculus gracilis (carrying lower limb signals) is not present at cervical levels and therefore cannot be damaged there. This is diagnostically critical: cervical cord injury does not directly disrupt lower limb proprioception at the cervical level itself — gracilis contributions to proprioception are only at risk for thoracic and lumbar cord lesions.
