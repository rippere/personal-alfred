# Tanycytes Constitute a Selective Blood-to-CSF Transport Interface Distinct from BBB-Absent CVOs

## Claim
Tanycytes are a specialized ependymal cell subtype that actively regulate molecular transport from blood into CSF and distribute signaling molecules to brain tissue. Unlike circumventricular organs (which passively sample blood by lacking the BBB entirely), tanycytes constitute an active, selective gating mechanism for blood-derived signals entering the CSF compartment.

## Basis
Covered in NEURO 302 glia lecture (2026-01-30). Three ependymal cell subtypes enumerated: choroid plexus cells (CSF production), ependymocytes (CSF circulation and reabsorption via cilia and microvilli), and tanycytes (blood-to-CSF regulation, molecule distribution to brain).

## Evidence Trail
- 2026-01-30: Tanycytes listed as distinct ependymal subtype with specific regulatory function, separate from ependymocytes.

## Impact
Tanycytes represent a third mechanism for blood-CNS communication, alongside (1) standard BBB-intact capillaries (exclusion) and (2) CVO fenestrated capillaries (free passage). Their selective transport function is clinically relevant for hypothalamic signaling, metabolic regulation (e.g., leptin and energy homeostasis), and CNS drug delivery strategies targeting the CSF route.
