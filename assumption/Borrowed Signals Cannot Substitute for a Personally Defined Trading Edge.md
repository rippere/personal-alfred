# Borrowed Signals Cannot Substitute for a Personally Defined Trading Edge

## Claim

Relying on external call groups or third-party technical analysis signals — rather than a self-defined and strictly followed edge — produces poor trading outcomes. An edge must be personally defined to be executable and compounding.

## Basis

October 2025 journal: Ben concluded his TA was not working and was being "propped up" by a call group that was also ineffective. Cost-benefit analysis led to a binary conclusion: either quit until properly equipped, or strictly define and follow a personal edge. The call group dependency had been masking the absence of a real edge.

## Evidence Trail

- Oct 23 2025 journal: "TA is trash unless I'm following edge strictly. Edge has been unable to be defined by me yet."
- Call group providing cover for undefined personal edge — recognized as anti-pattern
- Decision: either quit until equipped, or define and follow own edge strictly

## Impact

Directly informs the trading edge mental model that later generalizes to career niche strategy: borrowed expertise signals don't compound the way self-defined edges do. A person operating on someone else's framework cannot iterate or improve it.
