# Astrocytes Are Functionally Differentiated Into Protoplasmic and Fibrous Subtypes by CNS Compartment

## Claim
Astrocytes — the most abundant CNS glial cell (GFAP+) — exist in two functionally distinct subtypes determined by their anatomical compartment. Protoplasmic astrocytes (gray matter) perform synaptic regulation via the tripartite synapse, glutamate and ion clearance, BBB maintenance, synaptic pruning, and synaptogenesis. Fibrous astrocytes (white matter) perform structural support, nourishment, and BBB maintenance but lack the tripartite synapse role.

## Basis
Covered in NEURO 302 lecture on cytoskeleton, glia, and cell diversity (2026-01-30). The functional differentiation reflects where each subtype operates: protoplasmic astrocytes embed in synaptic neuropil and participate in active signaling; fibrous astrocytes surround myelinated axon bundles and primarily provide structural and metabolic support.

## Evidence Trail
- 2026-01-30: Protoplasmic listed with BBB, tripartite synapse, glutamate/ion clearance, synaptic pruning, synaptogenesis. Fibrous listed with BBB, structural support, nourishment.

## Impact
The tripartite synapse model (neuron-neuron-astrocyte) only applies in gray matter — white matter astrocytes are purely homeostatic/structural. Clinical significance: gray vs white matter astrocyte dysfunction produce distinct functional deficits. Relevant to MS (predominantly white matter) vs disorders affecting synaptic regulation (predominantly gray matter astrocyte pathology).
