# Brain Metabolic Demand Vastly Exceeds Its Proportional Mass

## Claim
Despite constituting only ~2% of total body mass, the brain consumes approximately 15% of resting cardiac output and 25% of the body's total oxygen supply. This extreme metabolic asymmetry means any interruption to cerebral blood flow produces rapid, severe functional consequences.

## Basis
Directly stated in NEURO 404 lecture on blood supply of the brain (2026-02-09). The figures (2% / 15% / 25%) are empirical measurements of cerebral metabolic demand at rest.

## Evidence Trail
- 2026-02-09: Stated in lecture as foundational context for understanding cerebrovascular anatomy and stroke significance.

## Impact
Explains why stroke outcomes are so severe relative to vascular events elsewhere, why the Circle of Willis redundancy evolved, and why fMRI BOLD signal is a reliable proxy for neural activity — blood flow tracks metabolic demand closely.
