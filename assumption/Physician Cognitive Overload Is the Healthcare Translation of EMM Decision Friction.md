# Physician Cognitive Overload Is the Healthcare Translation of EMM Decision Friction

## Claim

Physician cognitive overload — the well-documented organizational phenomenon where physicians face too many complex decisions with insufficient structure — is the healthcare domain's direct translation of EMM's core problem: decision friction. EMM's value proposition requires no repositioning for healthcare; the problem is the same problem described in a different professional language.

## Basis

Healthcare Executive Meeting Prep analysis (2026-02-24) identified decision friction ↔ physician cognitive overload as a direct ley line connecting EMM's core domain to healthcare executive concerns. The mapping was identified alongside three other cross-domain bridges: behavioral finance ↔ population health, fraternity management ↔ physician management, and AI PM trajectory ↔ healthcare systems.

## Evidence Trail

- 2026-02-24: Identified as a direct domain mapping during healthcare executive meeting prep with Claude Code

## Impact

If this maps correctly, EMM's pitch to healthcare organizations leads with the problem (cognitive overload, decision paralysis at the physician level) before introducing the product. This is a stronger opening than leading with features. Also supports the hypothesis that EMM's problem domain is domain-agnostic and the product requires only language translation per vertical.
