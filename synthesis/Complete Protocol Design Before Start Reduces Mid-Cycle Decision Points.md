# Complete Protocol Design Before Start Reduces Mid-Cycle Decision Points

## Insight
The entire April 2026 GH optimization protocol — reconstitution math, dosing schedule, supply timeline, and all stacking decisions — was finalized in a single session before the first injection. This front-loaded approach resolved logistics (reconstitution concentrations, syringe volume compatibility, supply exhaustion dates) and strategy (GHRH class selection, dosing duration trade-offs, timing windows) before beginning.

## Evidence
- April 14 2026 session: "reconstitution math, dosing schedule, duration estimates" all completed in one sitting
- Five distinct decisions finalized pre-start: Tesamorelin dose and split timing, CJC-1295 elimination, MOTS-c dose revision, GHK-Cu reconstitution concentration, Selank delivery route
- Supply restock schedule (GHK-Cu week 7, Tesamorelin week 9) calculated and documented before protocol began

## Implications
Future protocols should batch design decisions into a single pre-start session. Mid-cycle dosing changes confound results — it becomes impossible to attribute observed effects to specific agents. Complete upfront planning also surfaces logistical constraints (syringe volume limits, 30-day reconstitution windows) before they become emergencies.

## Applicability
Any multi-peptide protocol where outcome attribution matters. Most applicable when protocol duration exceeds 4 weeks and involves multiple interacting agents with interdependent logistics.
